ICAM.Install

June 16, 2016 / Senior Year Project / Development and Implementation

ICAM.install celebrates the imagination and innovation of ICAM's undergraduate seniors. The diverse and broad range of artwork featured includes everything from mobile apps and video games, to sculpture and photography. Students explore the possibilities of current technology like 3D printing, EEG headsets, computer vision, robotics, and holograms. This is the epitome of ICAMâ€“to challenge the conventions of art and technology.

Initial Ideas

ICAM.install was the Senior Thesis Show for the 2016 Interdisciplinary Computing and the Arts graduating students presented at UCSD's Adam D. Kamil Gallery. I worked with another student along with faculty and staff to provide the best platform for students to present their work. We collaborated with the students and met a diverse range of needs, considering space, time, and resource limitations. Together, we gathered and provided appropriate documentation to meet deadlines and properly manage the space.
